QDESIGN ANNOUNCES RAVESOUND FOR UNRIVALED AUDIO QUALITY AT LOW DATA RATES


RaveSound Technology Delivers 44.1kHz Stereo over 28.8K Modem

Vancouver, Canada (January 6, 1998) - QDesign Corporation announced the release of RaveSound, the company's new breakthrough audio compression technology targeted at delivering high quality audio for very low bitrate applications such as the Internet. RaveSound, the technology behind the QDesign Music Codec ("QDMC") has been licensedby Apple Computer, Inc. and is integrated into QuickTime 3.0, the latestversion of Apple's award-winning, industry-standard software architecture for creating and publishing digital media for Mac OS and Windows.

RaveSound represents a fundamentally new approach in audio coding that enables it to reduce an audio file to as little as one percent of its original file size while maintaining audio fidelity. Unlike MPEG or Dolby AC-3,RaveSound is optimized for producing full bandwidth, 44.1kHz stereo soundfor delivery over a 28.8K modem.

"QDesign is committed to providing the highest possible audio qualityto meet the needs of the entire spectrum of audio for new media application.",said Richard J. Beaton, president and founder of QDesign. "We developed the world's first MPEG-2 stereo compression software for broadcasters becauseof the demand for a software-only implementation of the standard that would provide broadcasters with a flexible and cost-effective means for audio storage and transmission. MPEG was designed to provide CD-quality sound for studio and broadcast applications at higher bitrates. Bandwidth-limited Internet applications, however, demand a fundamentally different approach to achieve high quality audio at modem data rates. The RaveSound algorithmbuilds a model of the signal, separating the signal into perceptually-relevant'audio building blocks' which can be efficiently encoded for transmissionor storage. This model allows RaveSound to minimize audible distortionwell below the bitrates attained by perceptual coding systems such as MPEG,and makes it possible to achieve truly high quality audio at extraordinarily low data rates."

The QDesign Music Codec ("QDMC") is the QuickTime implementation of the RaveSound technology. The QDMC decoder and an "SE" version of the encoder have been licensed to Apple Computer for integration into QuickTime 3.0 as the Internet audio solution for QuickTime developers. A Developer Preview Release is available for immediate download at http://www.apple.com/quicktime/preview.

"The licensing of QDMC to Apple represents a strategic move onQDesign's part to ensure the RaveSound technology gets to developers who value high quality content most, the QuickTime developers," said Mandy Chan, director of marketing for QDesign. "QuickTime is the standardplatform for media publishing and playback. QuickTime has already handledthe challenges of media synchronization and packaging. Now that QDMC isa standard audio format in QuickTime, developers will be able to add voice,sound effects, and music to any video, animation or graphics media formatsupported by QuickTime for truly compelling websites and presentationsthat will playback across Mac OS and Windows."

With the support of QDMC in QuickTime, the RaveSound technology establishes itself as a standard for all applications and media devices that require high quality audio. QDesign expects that QuickTime 3.0 will be the ubiquitous media player of choice.

The RaveSound technology is available on both the Mac OS and Windows platform. For details regarding technology licensing, please contact QDesign at enquiries@qdesign.com.

About QDesign Corporation: Based in Vancouver, Canada, QDesign is a privately held company which develops and markets state-of-the-art audio compression technologies tothe broadcast and new media industry. The company introduced the world's first real-time, studio-quality MPEG-2 stereo compression software in October1996; making available for the first time, high-quality, high-performance MPEG sound to all broadcast and studio professionals. QDesign is pioneering another breakthrough in audio compression with the QDesign Music Codec technology for Internet and new media applications. QDesign customers include internationalbroadcasters such as Associated Press, BBC, Canadian Broadcasting Corporation(CBC), CBS News, and Sony Worldwide Networks. QDesign technology has been used by multimedia producers such as Broderbund and in new media applications from innovators such as Iomega. Visit QDesign's home page at www.qdesign.com.
